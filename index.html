PROTOCOL NOTES

💉 PRE-WORKOUT PROTOCOL

  • Intra-workout: 1.5L water + high-dose electrolytes + 3g Taurine (prevents Anavar pumps/cramps)
  • Post-workout: 15–20 min Ice Barrel or Sauna at Alpha Gym (inflammation management)

🔄 ENHANCED REST-PAUSE TECHNIQUE

Applied to the final set of every exercise. The gold checkboxes in each exercise card guide you through it:

  • Perform working set to failure
  • Rest exactly 15 seconds
  • Perform AMRAP (as many reps as possible) at same weight
  • Rest exactly 15 seconds
  • Final "burnout" mini-set — everything you have left
  • Achieves double-workout stimulus in 1/10th the time

🧬 PEPTIDE SYNERGY — ECCENTRIC PROTOCOL

  • BPC-157 + TB-500 are actively remodeling your tendons and connective tissue
  • Apply 3-second negatives (eccentrics) on EVERY rep to maximize this synergy
  • This means: lower the weight slowly — count 1…2…3 on every descent
  • Machine Preacher Curl: avoid full dead-stop at bottom to protect tendons during remodeling

❤️ BLOOD PRESSURE SAFETY (TRT + ANAVAR)

  • No 1-rep max testing. Ever. Minimum rep floor is 8 reps.
  • If heart rate does NOT drop below 100bpm during rest periods — extend rest or reduce weight
  • The BP Alert button (red ❤️ button on workout days) triggers the safety protocol reminder
  • Anavar increases hematocrit — monitor for headache, vision changes, or unusual flushing
  • Age factor (55): prioritize joint longevity over maximal load — the protocol handles the hypertrophy

🔗 GYM80 CABLE ART — HOW TO USE

  • Alpha Gym runs the gym80 international full lineup — German-engineered, one of the best in the world
  • The Cable Art / Cable Crossover is the dual adjustable pulley station — set height per exercise
  • Cables beat machines for some exercises because tension is constant throughout full ROM — no drop-off at peak
  • Cable Tricep Pushdown: rope attachment, high cable, elbows pinned to ribs, split the rope ends at the bottom
  • Cable Overhead Tricep: rope, low cable, face away — hits the long head (largest tricep portion)
  • Cable Lat Pulldown: high cable, wide bar — superior ROM and tension vs selectorized machine
  • Cable Face Pull: high cable, rope — best exercise for rear delts AND shoulder health in one movement
  • Cable Crossover Fly: both cables at shoulder height, D-handles — squeeze 2s at centre

🧱 DECLINE BENCH ABS — EVERY SESSION

  • Decline increases ROM vs flat crunch — greater stretch + greater contraction = more muscle fibre activation
  • Day 1: 3 × 15–20 bodyweight or light plate — warm up the core post-push
  • Day 2: 3 × 15–20 bodyweight or plate — consistent volume on pull day
  • Day 4: 4 × 15–20 with progressive weight — Friday is the heavy ab day, increase plate load weekly
  • Progression: BW → 5kg plate → 10kg plate → 15kg plate across chest as 20 reps becomes easy
  • With TRT + Anavar: core recovery is fast. If energy is high, add a 4th set on Days 1 & 2 too
  • Form: chin to chest, elbows wide, squeeze for 1 second at top, 3-second eccentric descent
  • Padel is NOT casual exercise — it's a primary training stimulus and fat-mobilization tool
  • No direct quad or hamstring isolation in lifting — CNS and legs are preserved FOR padel
  • Hip Abduction Machine on Day 4 directly builds padel lateral stability
  • 45° Back Extension (glute focus) protects the court movement pattern

⚖️ THE ANTI-LEG RULE

No leg press, leg extension, leg curl, or squats — intentionally. Your CNS and local leg recovery are reserved entirely for padel performance and glute-specific power work. The glute machines (hip thrust, hip abduction, back extension) give you the glute development without competing with padel recovery.

🦾 TENNIS ELBOW REHAB PROTOCOL

Lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow) is a degenerative tendinopathy of the ECRB tendon at the lateral epicondyle — caused by the repetitive wrist extension and pronation of Padel strokes. Daily eccentric loading is the #1 evidence-based treatment. BPC-157 + TB-500 will accelerate collagen remodeling significantly — you are well-positioned to heal this fast.

  • Eccentric Wrist Extension — the core exercise. Use good hand to lift, affected arm lowers over 4 seconds. 3×15 daily. Start 0.5kg, progress only when pain-free.
  • Wrist Extension Stretch — arm straight, palm down, wrist dropped, pull toward body. 3 × 30–45s hold. Do before AND after every Padel match.
  • Forearm Supination / Pronation — builds the rotator chain stability that prevents overload on the lateral epicondyle during Padel strokes. 3×15, 2s each direction.
  • Timeline — expect meaningful pain reduction at 4–6 weeks of daily work. Full resolution typically 8–12 weeks. Do not skip days.
  • Padel mechanics — check your grip size and backhand technique. A grip that's too small is a primary driver of tennis elbow in racket sports.
  • Avoid — no corticosteroid injections (worsens long-term outcomes). Palm-down heavy lifting (like rows) — keep using palm-up or neutral grip where possible.
